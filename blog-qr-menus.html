5 Ways Interactive QR Menus Increase Your Drink Cross-Sells

While standard PDF menus opened via QR code proved their worth during the pandemic, they leave critical revenue on the table. A truly interactive digital menu acts as a silent salesperson, upselling intelligently without seeming pushy.

1. Visually Stimulating Imagery

A textual description of a "Smoked Old Fashioned" is nice, but a high-definition image of the drink smoking under a glass cloche triggers cravings. Beautiful UI encourages impulsive add-ons.

"Our specialty cocktail sales jumped 40% when we switched from a text list to a visual grid."

2. Contextual Pairings

When a guest adds a steak to their cart, an interactive QR menu can instantly suggest a paired Cabernet Sauvignon. By doing the thinking for the customer, you remove friction from the upselling process.

3. One-Tap Reordering

The hardest drink to sell is the second one. If a guest has to catch a busy waiter's eye, they might just ask for the check instead. With an interactive menu left open on their phone, repeating a drink order takes a single tap.

4. Highlighting High-Margin Items

Unlike printed menus, digital menus can be reorganized on the fly. Running a promotion on a high-margin draft beer? Pin it to the top of the menu instantly across every table.

5. Custom Modifications

Digital ordering reduces the anxiety of asking for complex modifications, resulting in higher satisfaction and willingness to pay premium prices for add-ons.
